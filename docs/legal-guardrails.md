# Legal Guardrails

## Purpose

This project is an MIT-licensed payments tool, not a trading campaign. Its job
is to make Dogecoin easier to use, easier to verify, and easier to evaluate
without promising returns or trying to force market action.

## Required Rules

- No coordinated buying, selling, holding, or timed trading pushes.
- No wash trading, spoofing, fake receipts, fake merchant listings, or fake
  user counts.
- No undisclosed compensation, paid influencer activity, or hidden treasury
  support.
- No guaranteed-return language, target-price promises, or "can’t lose"
  framing.
- No custody of third-party funds unless the operator is licensed and prepared
  for the regulatory burden.
- No deceptive press claims, selective data editing, or suppression of failed
  pilots.

## Allowed Tactics

- Publish open-source tooling for payment links, QR invoices, merchant badges,
  and reporting.
- Recruit merchants, creators, and nonprofits to run opt-in pilots with clear
  disclosures.
- Report anonymized transaction counts, DOGE volume, retention, and methodology.
- Use sponsorship language that is plain, prominent, and archived.
- Pitch processors and ecommerce tools using measured utility data.

## Messaging Standard

Every public message should pass these tests:

1. A reasonable reader can tell whether the message is a goal, a current fact,
   or a hypothetical scenario.
2. Any paid promotion or financial interest is disclosed in the same post,
   video, or page.
3. The message asks people to use or evaluate the product, not to trade the
   asset.
4. The numbers shown can be traced back to a source or to the project's own
   documented methodology.

## Release Gate

Do not ship a public asset unless it:

- avoids price-prediction language in calls to action,
- includes sponsor and holdings disclosure where relevant,
- can be supported by evidence,
- and points users toward real usage rather than speculation.
