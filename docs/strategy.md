# DOGE2MOON Strategy

## Objective

Increase real-world Dogecoin adoption through public, repeatable, legally
defensible demand. The campaign should make Dogecoin more useful, easier to
accept, and easier to measure. The `$1.00` figure is treated only as a market
scenario, not as a public promise or campaign message.

No plan can guarantee a market price. The strongest ethical approach is
adoption with proof.

## Constraints

- No coordinated pump, wash trading, fake volume, spoofing, or market deception.
- No undisclosed paid promotion.
- No guaranteed-return language.
- No custody of user funds.
- No claims that merchant adoption alone guarantees `$1.00`.
- No calls to buy, hold, or trade DOGE as part of campaign participation.

## Thesis

Dogecoin already has recognizability, low-friction payments, and a culture built
around "Do Only Good Everyday." The missing piece is concentrated proof: visible
places where people can spend DOGE, creators can receive DOGE, nonprofits can
report DOGE donations, and the public can see real transaction counts.

Official Dogecoin materials describe Dogecoin as open-source peer-to-peer
digital currency and emphasize its community, wallets, low fees, and charitable
history. The Foundation tagline is "Do Only Good Everyday."

## Six-Month Execution

### Month 1 - Proof Before Hype

Goal: 50 merchants, 100 creators, 10 nonprofits, first public proof report.

Actions:

- Launch the app as the public adoption desk.
- Recruit merchants with low operational risk: coffee, food trucks, online
  creators, digital goods, small events, local services.
- Give each merchant a payment URI, QR code, badge, and public pledge listing.
- Ask for transaction counts and aggregate DOGE volume only, not customer data.
- Publish a weekly proof report.

### Month 2 - City Clusters

Goal: 5 visible districts with at least 25 DOGE-accepting merchants each.

Actions:

- Concentrate activity geographically so users can spend DOGE twice in one day.
- Pair merchants with local creators who can demonstrate real purchases.
- Run public receipt-count challenges with no buy coordination.

### Month 3 - Nonprofit Rails

Goal: 50 nonprofit donation pages and 5 public impact reports.

Actions:

- Provide a donation QR and reporting template.
- Create matching pools funded transparently.
- Build credibility through receipts, not price language.

### Month 4 - Payment Processor Push

Goal: 3 integration conversations and 1 pilot with an ecommerce or loyalty tool.

Actions:

- Use merchant transaction proof to approach processors.
- Prioritize plugins and payment links that do not require merchants to manage
  private keys if they do not want to.
- Keep DOGE as payment utility, not speculative messaging.

### Month 5 - Retention And Repeat Use

Goal: 30 percent merchant retention and 25 percent repeat payer rate in pilot
locations.

Actions:

- Add loyalty offers priced in DOGE.
- Encourage creator subscriptions and recurring tips.
- Publish repeat-rate metrics alongside new merchant counts.

### Month 6 - Public Proof Report

Goal: a rigorous adoption report strong enough for partners, press, and payment
companies.

Actions:

- Publish merchant count, transaction count, DOGE volume, retention, repeat
  payer estimates, campaign spend, sponsorship disclosures, and methodology.
- Include failures and churn reasons.
- Turn the best merchant stories into partner pitches.

## Why This Can Matter

The market cap implied by a hypothetical `$1.00` scenario is far above the
current baseline. That means the campaign needs more than memes. It needs a
proof flywheel:

1. More places accept DOGE.
2. More people see DOGE used.
3. More creators and nonprofits normalize payment links.
4. More processors see merchant demand.
5. More integrations reduce friction.
6. More legitimate demand improves the case for broader long-term relevance.

## Highest-Leverage Bets

- Concentrated merchant clusters beat scattered logos.
- Public proof reports beat slogans.
- Creator tipping beats passive holding narratives.
- Nonprofit transparency fits Dogecoin culture.
- Payment processor pilots scale faster than one-by-one onboarding.

## Release Criteria

The outcome is strong enough to release when:

- At least 50 merchants can be onboarded with the provided assets.
- Every public claim can be verified or clearly labeled as a goal.
- The app can generate payment URIs and pledge exports.
- The first proof report template is ready.
- The campaign has a rulebook against manipulation and undisclosed promotion.
- Public-facing assets pass the legal guardrails in `docs/legal-guardrails.md`.
