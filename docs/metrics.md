# Metrics And Proof Rules

## North Star

Verified DOGE payment count per month.

Price can move for reasons outside the campaign. Usage is the metric the
campaign can actually influence.

## Core Metrics

- Merchant pledges.
- Active DOGE-accepting merchants.
- Verified DOGE transactions.
- Estimated DOGE payment volume.
- Repeat payer rate.
- Merchant retention after 30 and 90 days.
- Creator DOGE tip links created.
- Nonprofit donation pages launched.
- Payment processor conversations and pilots.
- Sponsorship dollars and disclosures.

## Six-Month Targets

- 1,000 merchant pledges.
- 250 active merchants.
- 100 creators using DOGE tip links.
- 50 nonprofit donation pages.
- 50,000 verified DOGE transactions.
- 30 percent merchant retention after 90 days.
- 3 processor or ecommerce integration pilots.

These are ambitious launch targets, not price guarantees.

## Report Format

Every proof report should include:

- Date range.
- Merchant count at start and end.
- New merchants.
- Churned merchants.
- Transaction count.
- DOGE volume.
- USD estimate and price source.
- Repeat payer estimate.
- Campaign spend.
- Paid promotion list.
- Methodology.
- Known limitations.

## Verification Levels

Level 1: self-reported pledge.

Level 2: public DOGE payment page or QR visible.

Level 3: merchant shares anonymized transaction count.

Level 4: transaction count corroborated with public address activity or payment
processor export.

Level 5: retained merchant with repeat payer evidence.

## Anti-Manipulation Guardrails

- Never ask supporters to buy at the same time.
- Never report self-dealing transactions as organic usage.
- Never pay merchants to fake transactions.
- Never hide paid creator relationships.
- Never present modeled demand as actual demand.
- Never imply `$1.00` is guaranteed.

## Data Dictionary

`merchant_pledge`: a merchant that publicly agrees to test DOGE acceptance.

`active_merchant`: a merchant with a live DOGE payment method.

`verified_transaction`: a DOGE payment observed through merchant reporting,
processor export, or address activity.

`repeat_payer`: a payer believed to have made more than one DOGE payment within
the reporting window, measured only through privacy-preserving aggregate data.
