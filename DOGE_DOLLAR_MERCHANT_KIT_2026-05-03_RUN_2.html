Make DOGE useful before asking it to be valuable.

Current context: DOGE was near $0.106 with roughly $18B market cap in recent CoinMarketCap data. A $1 target implies a step-change in demand, so this sprint focuses on repeatable merchant acceptance, creator commerce, and spendable utility.

Strategic wedge

  • 1Recruit merchants whose buyers already overlap with DOGE culture: gaming, creator merch, sports communities, collectibles, food trucks, local services, and independent ecommerce.
  • 2Lead with chargeback reduction, new buyer reach, daily settlement, and brand participation. Do not lead with price speculation.
  • 3Use existing rails first: BitPay for compliant hosted checkout, NOWPayments for non-custodial gateway needs, GigaWallet for teams that can self-host DOGE infrastructure.
  • 4Ask the DOGE community to spend small amounts at verified merchants and post proof of real purchases, not buy pressure.

Merchant onboarding

A concrete checklist for a business that wants DOGE payments with minimal operational risk.

Hosted checkout path

  • 1Pick a payment provider that currently supports DOGE invoice payments in the merchant's region.
  • 2Set settlement preference before launch: local currency for lower volatility, DOGE for treasury exposure, or a defined mix.
  • 3Create a DOGE payment link or checkout integration and run two internal test orders.
  • 4Add a checkout badge, help-center note, refund policy, and tax/accounting workflow.
  • 5Launch with a capped proof campaign: first 50 DOGE orders, public receipts only if customers opt in.
