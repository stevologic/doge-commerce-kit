# DOGE Proof Engine Operator Playbook

## Mission

Create a repeatable proof loop for DOGE commerce. Every valid purchase should produce one merchant-facing result: a usable proof post, a case-study input, or a data point that improves the next merchant pitch.

## Daily Workflow

1. Collect new proof submissions from buyers, merchants, and creators.
2. Remove or redact personal information before internal sharing.
3. Confirm the DOGE payment route: direct DOGE, BitPay, NOWPayments, GigaWallet/self-hosted, or clearly labeled gift-card route.
4. Score each proof record in `index.html`.
5. Publish only records marked `ready`.
6. Add daily totals to the campaign KPI tracker.
7. Send the top proof post to merchant prospects that match the same category.

## Proof Scoring Rules

Start from zero and add:

- 20 points: merchant checkout route confirmed.
- 15 points: screenshot or receipt is redacted and publishable.
- 15 points: DOGE amount and USD value are both present.
- 15 points: product or offer is specific.
- 15 points: buyer or merchant permits public use.
- 10 points: category matches a high-fit segment.
- 10 points: route is direct DOGE or DOGE invoice rather than an indirect gift-card route.

Publishing threshold:

- 80-100: publish immediately after final privacy check.
- 60-79: usable after one missing item is fixed.
- 40-59: keep for internal metrics only.
- below 40: reject or request resubmission.

## Publishing Rules

Use adoption language:

- "DOGE was used to buy..."
- "This merchant accepted DOGE for..."
- "Real checkout proof from DOGE Spend Week..."
- "This is why merchants should test one DOGE-ready offer."

Avoid market language:

- no price predictions,
- no guaranteed appreciation claims,
- no "buy now before it pumps",
- no calls for coordinated buying,
- no misleading partnership claims.

## Weekly Report Format

Publish a short report every seven days:

- active merchants,
- orders submitted,
- verified proof artifacts,
- total reported USD value,
- total reported DOGE volume,
- top three merchant categories,
- three anonymized buyer quotes,
- next week's merchant recruitment target.

## Merchant Recruitment Follow-Up

Send proof by category. A gaming proof post should go to game servers and indie game sellers. A creator merch proof post should go to creators and storefront providers. The point is to make the pitch feel operationally close to the prospect's business.

## Escalation Path

If proof volume is low, narrow the campaign:

1. Pick one category.
2. Recruit five sellers.
3. Run one 72-hour drop.
4. Publish every valid proof artifact.
5. Use those artifacts to recruit the next ten sellers.

Broad campaigns without proof become abstract promotion. Narrow campaigns with proof become sales collateral.
