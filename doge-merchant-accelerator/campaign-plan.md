# Six-Month DOGE Adoption Campaign Plan

This plan is designed for legal adoption and promotion only. It does not depend on coordinated trading, price guarantees, undisclosed sponsorships, bots, or deceptive claims.

## Strategic Target

Current reference from CoinMarketCap on 2026-05-03 showed DOGE around `$0.106` with roughly `169.7B` DOGE circulating supply. A `$1` price would imply about `$170B` market value, so the campaign has to create visible utility, outside attention, and repeated transaction demand. Promotion without spendable use is not enough.

## North Star

Build a public, verifiable DOGE merchant network that makes DOGE feel normal to spend.

Primary metrics:

- New merchants accepting DOGE.
- DOGE checkout transactions.
- Repeat DOGE customer purchases.
- Merchant retention after the first campaign.
- Earned media mentions tied to usage, not price.

## Month 1: Proof Sprint

Goal: get the first 10 merchants live with documented DOGE offers.

Actions:

- Target internet-native merchants: coffee, creator merch, gaming accessories, collectibles, hot sauce, streetwear, events, indie software, and local food trucks.
- Offer a simple launch kit: payment setup checklist, badge, launch post, volatility disclosure, and recap template.
- Ask for one narrow offer per merchant.
- Publish weekly adoption recaps with merchant permission.

Success threshold:

- 10 live merchant pages.
- 250 combined DOGE checkout attempts.
- 3 merchant testimonials focused on setup and customer interest.

## Month 2: Category Clusters

Goal: make DOGE acceptance visible inside specific communities.

Actions:

- Build three merchant clusters of 8 to 12 stores each.
- Prioritize categories with shareable products and repeat purchases.
- Recruit one creator per cluster to demonstrate real checkout.
- Create cluster pages: "Spend DOGE on coffee", "Spend DOGE on creator merch", "Spend DOGE on gaming gear".

Success threshold:

- 35 total merchants.
- 2,000 combined DOGE checkout attempts.
- At least 10 merchants keep DOGE enabled after launch.

## Month 3: Public Proof and Press

Goal: convert adoption data into mainstream coverage.

Actions:

- Publish a transparent adoption report.
- Pitch local business press, crypto press, creator economy writers, and payments newsletters.
- Package merchant stories around customer acquisition and checkout learning.
- Avoid price framing in all press materials.

Success threshold:

- 10 earned media mentions.
- 75 total merchants.
- 7,500 DOGE checkout attempts.

## Month 4: Merchant Referral Engine

Goal: let merchants recruit merchants.

Actions:

- Launch a referral page for merchants who want the DOGE kit.
- Give existing merchants co-marketing visibility for referring another store.
- Publish setup videos and processor-specific guides.
- Start weekly office hours.

Success threshold:

- 150 total merchants.
- 25,000 DOGE checkout attempts.
- 40 percent of new merchants from referrals.

## Month 5: DOGE Spend Week

Goal: coordinate utility, not trading.

Actions:

- Run a week where merchants offer DOGE-specific products or perks.
- Publish a live directory of offers.
- Use creators to show products and checkout flows.
- Encourage customers to buy only things they actually want.

Success threshold:

- 300 total merchants.
- 100,000 DOGE checkout attempts.
- 20 public merchant case studies.

## Month 6: Institutional-Grade Adoption Report

Goal: produce the most credible adoption artifact possible.

Actions:

- Release a report with merchant counts, anonymized transaction ranges, retention, checkout lessons, customer survey results, and compliance practices.
- Use the report to pitch payment processors, ecommerce platforms, and larger merchants.
- Ask processors to create DOGE-specific onboarding pages.

Success threshold:

- 500 total merchants.
- 250,000 DOGE checkout attempts.
- 50 retained merchants with repeat DOGE volume.

## Why This Could Matter

DOGE has cultural awareness but needs repeated practical use. If a large public merchant network forms quickly, it can produce legitimate attention from customers, creators, press, and payment companies. That is the clean path: more places to spend, more reasons to transact, more credible adoption stories.

This still cannot guarantee a `$1` market price. It is the strongest legally defensible route because it grows utility instead of trying to manufacture price movement.
