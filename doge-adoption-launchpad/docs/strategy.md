# Strategy

## Objective

Create a repeatable DOGE transaction engine that increases active wallets, merchant acceptance, recurring spend, and public visibility within six months.

The goal is not to "promise" a price target. The goal is to create the strongest legal demand-side mechanism available: adoption with measurable usage.

## Strategic thesis

Dogecoin reaches durable higher valuations when three conditions reinforce each other:

1. More places accept it.
2. More people actually use it.
3. More public proof exists that usage is growing.

Most crypto campaigns fail because they optimize for impressions instead of transactions. This package is built to optimize for transactions first, then convert those transactions into narrative.

## The wedge

Target small merchants and online sellers first.

Why:

- They adopt faster than large enterprises.
- They can run offers immediately.
- Their customers are reachable through local and niche communities.
- They generate visible proof quickly.

## Offer design

Every merchant launch should include one simple DOGE-only reason to buy:

- 10% off when paying in DOGE
- a limited "DOGE menu" or bundle
- one weekly DOGE special
- loyalty reward after three DOGE purchases
- a digital collectible, sticker, or status badge for DOGE customers

This converts DOGE from a passive holding asset into a spendable community token.

## Core program: Spend DOGE Week

Run a monthly activation called `Spend DOGE Week`.

Each activation includes:

1. Merchant recruitment
2. Consumer incentive
3. Local leaderboard
4. Creator challenge
5. Press and social proof recap

### Merchant recruitment

Recruit 25 to 100 merchants per wave across:

- coffee shops
- restaurants
- local service businesses
- independent retail
- niche online stores
- gaming communities
- digital goods sellers

### Consumer incentive

Customers need immediate benefit:

- discount for DOGE payments
- raffle entry for posting proof of purchase
- city-specific digital badge
- community prize pool funded by sponsors

### Local leaderboard

Publicly track:

- merchants activated
- DOGE transactions completed
- estimated DOGE spent
- repeat buyers

The scoreboard matters because people rally around visible progress.

### Creator challenge

Give creators a simple assignment:

- feature one merchant
- buy one item with DOGE
- post a short tutorial
- challenge followers to do the same

### Press recap

At the end of each wave, publish:

- number of participating merchants
- transaction count
- customer stories
- top cities or communities

Proof compounds trust.

## Six-month execution

### Month 1

- Publish landing page and merchant kit
- Recruit 10 pilot merchants
- Secure 5 creators and 2 sponsor partners
- Run one pilot city or one online-only vertical

### Month 2

- Refine onboarding from pilot friction
- Publish first case study
- Expand to 25 merchants
- Start weekly recap posts

### Month 3

- Launch first formal `Spend DOGE Week`
- Add leaderboard and simple submission form
- Recruit campus or meetup ambassadors

### Month 4

- Replicate in 3 cities or 3 online verticals
- Add sponsor-funded reward pool
- Distribute merchant success clips aggressively

### Month 5

- Push for 100+ participating merchants
- Bundle creator contests with merchant drops
- Begin press outreach with transaction milestones

### Month 6

- Consolidate strongest markets
- Publish "DOGE works here" map and merchant directory
- Re-run best-performing offer formats
- Turn temporary campaign into ongoing program

## What to measure

Primary metrics:

- active participating merchants
- number of DOGE purchases
- repeat customer rate
- average DOGE transaction size
- total DOGE spent

Secondary metrics:

- merchant retention after 30 and 90 days
- creator conversion rates
- referral rate per merchant
- earned social impressions tied to real purchases

## Why this can move price

This works through legal market structure, not manipulation:

- new users acquire DOGE to spend
- merchants hold some DOGE or promote it
- repeated campaigns generate ongoing buy pressure
- visible usage changes public narrative from meme to money-in-motion

The strongest path to a higher price is not telling people to hold harder. It is building a reason for the next million people to acquire and use DOGE.

## Risks

- Too much focus on vanity metrics
- Merchants churn if setup is difficult
- Consumers ignore offers without urgency
- Community activity fragments without a central calendar

## Mitigations

- Optimize for completed transactions
- Standardize merchant onboarding
- Use limited-time offers
- Run recurring waves on a fixed schedule

## Standard

If an action does not increase merchant count, transaction count, repeat spend, or public proof of usage, deprioritize it.
